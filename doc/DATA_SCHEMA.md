# Econometric Codebook & Analytical Data Dictionary
## Dataset Architecture for OECD Pillar Two Tech Valuation Model

This document provides a comprehensive codebook and data schema mapping protocol for the micro-panel tracking matrices utilized inside the OLS, TWFE DiD, Batch SCM, and Causal Machine Learning pipelines.

## 1. Dataset Overview

* **Primary Logical Filename**: `analytical_panel_dataset.csv`
* **Data Dimension Balance**: Unbalanced Matrix Array.
* **Temporal Horizon Space**: Fiscal Years 2014 through 2026 inclusive (Chronological Cycle).
* **Total Sample Count**: 508 micro-panel entity observations.
* **Cross-Sectional Horizon Space (FY2025 Model Base)**: 93 distinct multinational technology corporations (MNEs).

## 3. Sampling Logic & GloBE Compliance Truncation

To construct an unbiased micro-panel tracking environment, raw corporate financial statements are filtered through strict truncation rules mimicking the OECD GloBE (Global Anti-Base Erosion) model statutory frameworks:

1. **Revenue Threshold Isolation**: Treated entities are selected based on the 750 Million Euro consolidated revenue threshold defined under Article 1.1 of the GloBE model rules.
2. **Missing Pattern Exclusion**: Observations exhibiting negative or missing `TotalRevenue`, or zero-variance `ETR` data rows are dropped to preserve matrix rank conditions during SLSQP minimization optimization loops.
3. **Outlier Winsorization**: Micro-covariates including `Leverage` and `RD_Intensity` are winsorized at the 1st and 99th percentiles to block anomalous reporting distortions from contaminating the cross-sectional random forest splits.

The singular matrix alert recorded in **Table 4 (Notes [2])** confirms a critical structural property of the tracking field: `Intangible_Ratio` shares an asymptotic perfect linear dependence with `RD_Intensity` and `ETR` among mega-cap tech conglomerates, which mathematically disables the classical OLS variance estimation. This behavior fully validates the necessity of applying the non-parametric **Module 3 (Causal ML Forest)**, which handles high-dimensional multicollinearity without design matrix constraints.
